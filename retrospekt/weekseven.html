-- Week 7 Reflection --

Week 7 had a introductory lecture on physical computing, one that really resonated with me during the lecture was James Auger and Jimmy Loizeau's interstitial which was a helmet that had a camera and microphone and required the user to stay in the helmet to interact with the world, the goal was to blur the lines between physical and virtual reality.

This idea really stuck with me: are we in a simulation? What do we know is real? These thoughts in mind had brought me back to the 1999 science fiction “The Matrix” and ultimately helped me settle on the idea of a matrix themed workbook for my 2C project.

Our hands-on session taught us the basics of an Arduino Kit. We made some basic circuits such as a light dimmer circuit which I have decided to 3D Model and implement into my workbook. Overall, these activities helped us identify components and their function which created a strong foundation for activities moving on.
